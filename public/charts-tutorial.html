📊 TradingView Tab

TradingView is an industry-standard charting platform used by professional traders worldwide. It's your primary tool for technical analysis, chart pattern recognition, and indicator-based research.

1

Search for a Stock Symbol

How to look up any stock, ETF, or crypto

Step-by-Step
  1. Look at the toolbar below the tabs — find the "Symbol" field on the left
  2. Click the input field (it shows "AAPL" by default)
  3. Type any ticker: SPY, TSLA, NVDA, QQQ, etc.
  4. A dropdown will appear with matching symbols. Click one to load it.
  5. Or type the full ticker and press Enter
💡 Tip: You can also search by company name — type "Apple" and you'll see AAPL in the dropdown.
2

Change the Timeframe

Switch between 1-minute, 5-minute, hourly, daily, and weekly views

Step-by-Step
  1. Find the "Timeframe" buttons in the toolbar: 1m, 5m, 15m, 1H, 1D, 1W, 1M
  2. Click any button — the chart reloads with that timeframe
  3. 1D (Daily) is the default and best for swing trading analysis
  4. 5m or 15m is best for day trading and intraday S.E.T. setups
  5. 1W (Weekly) is best for seeing the big picture trend
💡 Academy Tip: Module 3 (Master Surge Strategy) works best on the Daily timeframe. Module 7 (Futures) works best on 5m–15m for intraday trading.
3

Add Indicators (RSI, MACD, EMA, Volume)

Overlay technical indicators on your chart

Step-by-Step
  1. Find the "Indicators" chips in the toolbar: RSI, MACD, EMA, SMA, VWAP, Bollinger, Volume, ATR
  2. Click any chip to toggle it on (turns green when active)
  3. Click again to remove it
  4. For the Academy curriculum, the most important indicators are:
    • Volume — confirms institutional activity (Master Surge Pillar #2)
    • SMA or EMA — confirms trend direction (Pillar #4)
    • RSI — identifies overbought/oversold conditions
4

Use Drawing Tools (Built-in)

Draw support/resistance lines, trend lines, and rectangles directly on the chart

Step-by-Step
  1. Look at the left sidebar of the TradingView chart — it has drawing tool icons
  2. Click the line tool (diagonal line icon) to draw trend lines
  3. Click the horizontal line tool to mark price levels (perfect for S.E.T.)
  4. Click the rectangle tool to shade supply and demand zones
  5. Right-click any drawing to change its color (red for stop, yellow for entry, green for target)
  6. The top toolbar has undo/redo buttons if you make a mistake
💡 Pro Tip: TradingView auto-saves your drawings. When you come back later, all your lines and zones are still there.
🏋️ Practice Exercise: TradingView Basics
  1. Open the Live Charts page → click the TradingView tab
  2. Search for SPY and load its daily chart
  3. Add Volume and SMA indicators
  4. Use the horizontal line tool to mark today's high and low
  5. Switch to the 5-minute timeframe and observe intraday price action

📱 Webull Tab

Webull is your broker — where you actually place trades. The Webull tab connects you to their web trading platform so you can practice paper trading without leaving the Academy.

1

Open Webull from the Portal

Access Webull's full web trader in one click

Step-by-Step
  1. On the Live Charts page, click the "📱 Webull" tab
  2. You'll see the "Open Webull Web Trader" button
  3. Click it — Webull opens in a new browser tab
  4. Log in with your Webull account credentials
  5. You're now in Webull's full trading interface with charts, orders, portfolio, and options
💡 Why a new tab? Webull's security prevents embedding inside other sites (for your protection). The new tab gives you Webull's complete interface with zero limitations.
2

Enable Paper Trading

Switch to $1,000,000 virtual money mode

On Webull Web (app.webull.com)
  1. Click the menu icon (☰) in the top-left corner
  2. Find "Paper Trading" in the menu
  3. Toggle it ON
  4. Your balance changes to $1,000,000.00 — this is virtual money
  5. A "Paper" badge appears in the header confirming you're in simulation mode
⚠️ Critical: ALWAYS verify "Paper Trading" is active before placing any order. If you accidentally trade in live mode, you'll use real money.
3

Place Your First Paper Trade

Buy a stock with virtual money using a limit order

Step-by-Step
  1. Search for AAPL in Webull's search bar
  2. Click "Trade" on the stock page
  3. Set order type to "Limit" (NOT Market — we want our exact entry price)
  4. Set the limit price to your planned Entry price
  5. Set quantity to 10 shares (for your first practice trade)
  6. Click "Buy"
  7. The order will fill when the stock reaches your limit price
4

Set Stop Loss and Take Profit

Protect your trade with automatic exit orders

Setting a Stop Loss
  1. After your order fills, go to Positions
  2. Click your AAPL position
  3. Click "Close" or "Sell"
  4. Change order type to "Stop"
  5. Set the stop price to your planned Stop Loss level (1% below entry)
  6. Submit the order — it will trigger automatically if price drops to your stop
Setting a Take Profit
  1. Place another sell order on the same position
  2. Set order type to "Limit"
  3. Set the limit price to your Target (3× your risk above entry)
  4. Submit — it will trigger automatically when price reaches your target
✅ OCO Orders: Webull supports OCO (One-Cancels-Other) orders. Look for "OCO" in the order type dropdown — this sets both your stop loss AND take profit simultaneously. When one fills, the other auto-cancels. This is the most efficient way to manage your S.E.T.
5

Paper Trade Options

Practice buying call and put options with virtual money

Step-by-Step
  1. In Webull, go to Options (or search a stock → click "Options")
  2. You'll see the options chain: calls on the left, puts on the right
  3. Pick an expiration date at least 30 days out (less time decay pressure)
  4. Click a Call option near the current stock price (this is "at the money")
  5. Click "Trade" → set quantity to 1 contract (controls 100 shares)
  6. Note the premium — this is your max risk (perfect for the 1% rule)
  7. Click "Buy"
  8. Monitor: if the stock rises past your strike price, the option gains value
💡 Module 6 Connection: This is exactly what you learned in Module 6 (Options Deep Dive). 1 contract = 100 shares. Your max loss = the premium you paid. LEAPS are options expiring 1-3 years out.
🏋️ Practice Exercise: Webull Paper Trade
  1. Open the Webull tab → click "Open Webull Web Trader"
  2. Enable Paper Trading (verify the Paper badge)
  3. Search for SPY
  4. Place a Limit Buy order for 10 shares at the current price
  5. After it fills, set a Stop Loss 1% below your entry
  6. Set a Take Profit at 3% above your entry

🎯 Academy Charts Tab

Academy Charts is built specifically for the Impact Trading Academy. It has S.E.T. drawing tools, supply/demand zone marking, and an automatic position sizing calculator — features designed around your curriculum.

1

Load a Chart

Search for a symbol and select a timeframe

Step-by-Step
  1. Click the "🎯 Academy Charts" tab on the Live Charts page
  2. In the Symbol field, type a ticker (e.g., AAPL)
  3. Select a timeframe: 1m, 5m, 15m, 1H, 1D, 1W
  4. The chart loads with real market data from the Academy API
  5. The price overlay (top-left) shows the current price, change, and percentage
2

Place S.E.T. Lines (Stop, Entry, Target)

Draw your S.E.T. setup directly on the chart with color-coded lines

🛑
STOP
Red dashed line. Click the Stop button, then click on the chart where you want your stop loss.
🎯
ENTRY
Yellow solid line. Click the Entry button, then click on the chart at your planned entry price.
💰
TARGET
Green dashed line. Click the Target button, then click on the chart at your 3:1 target price.
Step-by-Step
  1. In the toolbar, find the "S.E.T. Tools" section
  2. Click "🛑 Stop" — the button turns red
  3. Click anywhere on the chart at the price level for your stop loss
  4. A red dashed line appears with "STOP" label on the price axis
  5. Repeat for "🎯 Entry" (yellow solid line) and "💰 Target" (green dashed line)
  6. Once all three are placed, the S.E.T. Analysis Panel appears on the right!
✅ Automatic Calculations: When all three S.E.T. lines are placed, the panel automatically calculates: Risk per share, Reward per share, R:R Ratio (color-coded: green if 3:1+, yellow if 2:1+, red if below), and Position Size for a $10,000 account at 1% risk.
3

Mark Supply & Demand Zones

Draw institutional footprint zones with two clicks

Step-by-Step
  1. In the toolbar, find the "Zones" section
  2. Click "📈 Demand" (for buy zones) or "📉 Supply" (for sell zones)
  3. First click on the chart sets the top boundary of the zone
  4. Second click sets the bottom boundary
  5. The zone appears as two labeled price lines (blue for demand, purple for supply)
  6. A legend appears in the bottom-left showing zone colors
💡 Module 2 Connection: Demand zones are where institutional buy orders are clustered (price bounced up from here before). Supply zones are where institutional sell orders are clustered. Look for areas of sharp price movement — that's the institutional footprint.
4

Add Indicators and Clear Drawings

Toggle SMA, EMA, Volume, and reset the chart

Indicators
  1. Find the "Indicators" section in the toolbar
  2. Click SMA — adds a blue 20-period Simple Moving Average line
  3. Click EMA — adds a yellow 9-period Exponential Moving Average line
  4. Click Vol — adds volume bars at the bottom (green = up candle, red = down candle)
  5. Click any active indicator again to remove it
Clearing
  1. Click "✕ Clear" to remove ALL drawings (S.E.T. lines + zones)
  2. The S.E.T. panel and zone legend will disappear
  3. Indicators stay — they're separate from drawings
🏋️ Practice Exercise: Academy Charts S.E.T.
  1. Open the Academy Charts tab → load AAPL daily chart
  2. Add Volume and SMA indicators
  3. Find a recent area where price bounced up → mark it as a Demand Zone
  4. Click "🛑 Stop" and place it just below the demand zone
  5. Click "🎯 Entry" and place it at the top of the demand zone
  6. Click "💰 Target" and place it at 3× the distance above entry
  7. Check the S.E.T. panel — is your R:R ratio 3:1 or better?

🎯 S.E.T. Practice Workflow

Now that you know all three tools, here's the complete workflow for analyzing a trade, planning your S.E.T., and executing it. This is how you use all three tabs together.

1

Step 1: Analyze on TradingView

Use TradingView's advanced tools to find your setup

The Analysis Phase
  1. Open the TradingView tab
  2. Load the stock you're interested in on the Daily timeframe
  3. Apply the 5 Pillars: Check the price cycle stage, look for volume surges, identify institutional footprints, confirm trend direction, plan risk management
  4. Use TradingView's drawing tools to mark potential supply/demand zones
  5. Switch to 15m or 1H to find your precise entry zone
2

Step 2: Plan S.E.T. on Academy Charts

Use the S.E.T. tools to calculate exact prices and position size

The Planning Phase
  1. Switch to the Academy Charts tab
  2. Load the same stock and timeframe
  3. Place your Stop (below demand zone / above supply zone)
  4. Place your Entry (at the zone boundary)
  5. Place your Target (3:1 reward-to-risk)
  6. Check the S.E.T. panel:
    • R:R should be 3:1 or better (green)
    • Note the position size (how many shares to trade)
    • Note the exact dollar amounts for risk and reward
3

Step 3: Execute on Webull

Place the actual paper trade with your calculated values

The Execution Phase
  1. Switch to the Webull tab → click "Open Webull Web Trader"
  2. Search for the same stock
  3. Place a Limit Buy at your Entry price
  4. Set quantity to the position size from the S.E.T. panel
  5. After fill → immediately set Stop Loss at your Stop price
  6. Set Take Profit at your Target price (or use OCO order for both at once)
  7. Let the trade manage itself — don't move your stop!
⚠️ Never move your stop. Once set, your stop loss stays where it is. Moving it wider means you're increasing risk beyond 1%. This is the #1 mistake that kills trader accounts.
4

Step 4: Review in Trade Journal

Log your results and learn from every trade

After the Trade
  1. Go to the Academy Trade Journal
  2. Log: symbol, entry, stop, target, position size, result (win/loss), P&L
  3. Note what the 5 Pillars told you vs. what actually happened
  4. After 10+ trades, review your win rate and average R:R
  5. Goal: 30%+ win rate with 3:1 R:R = consistent profitability
🏋️ Practice Exercise: Full S.E.T. Workflow
  1. TradingView: Find a stock with a volume surge on the daily chart
  2. Academy Charts: Mark a demand zone, then place S.E.T. lines — verify 3:1 R:R
  3. Webull: Execute the paper trade with your calculated position size
  4. Trade Journal: Log the trade details
  5. Wait for the trade to hit target or stop — then record the result

Module 2: Supply & Demand Zone Hunt

Identify 3 demand zones on SPY and paper trade one

Simulation Steps

Module 3: Master Surge Strategy

Apply all 5 Pillars to find and trade a surge setup

Simulation Steps

M4

Module 4: Position Sizing Drill

Calculate exact position sizes using the 1% rule

Simulation Steps
  1. Academy Charts: Load any stock. Place Stop and Entry.
  2. The S.E.T. panel automatically calculates: $10,000 × 1% = $100 max risk
  3. It divides $100 by the distance between Entry and Stop → exact share count
  4. Webull: Place the paper trade with that exact quantity
  5. After fill, verify: your dollar risk on the position matches 1% of $10,000 ($100)
  6. Repeat with 3 different stocks at different price levels
M6

Module 6: Options Paper Trade

Buy a call option and manage it with the S.E.T. rule

Simulation Steps
  1. TradingView: Analyze AAPL on the daily chart. Find a bullish setup.
  2. Webull: Go to Options → AAPL → Options Chain
  3. Pick a Call with 30+ days to expiration
  4. Choose a strike price at or slightly above current stock price
  5. Buy 1 contract — note the premium (this is your max risk)
  6. Set a sell target at 2-3× your premium
  7. Monitor daily. If the stock moves past your strike, the option gains value.
💡 Remember: 1 contract = 100 shares. A $3.00 premium costs $300 total. Your max loss is $300 — which may already satisfy the 1% rule on a $30,000 account.
