Place Your First Paper Trade

Execute a trade using the S.E.T. rule with virtual money
🔍

Step 1: Identify Your Setup

Use the chart you configured in Section 3. Find a demand zone (for longs) or supply zone (for shorts). Mark your S.E.T. levels with drawing tools. Verify the 3:1 reward-to-risk ratio.

🧮

Step 2: Calculate Position Size

Formula: Position Size = Account Risk / Trade Risk

Account Risk = $1,000,000 × 1% = $10,000

Trade Risk = Entry Price - Stop Price

Position Size = $10,000 / Trade Risk per share

🛒

Step 3: Place the Order

Click "Trade" on the stock. Select Limit Order (NOT market order — we want our exact entry). Set the limit price to your Entry price. Set the quantity to your calculated position size. Click Buy (for longs) or Sell (for shorts).

🛑

Step 4: Set Your Stop Loss

After your order fills, immediately place a Stop Loss order at your Stop price. In Webull: go to your position → click "Close" → select "Stop Order" → enter your stop price. This protects you at 1% max risk.

💰

Step 5: Set Your Take Profit

Place a Limit Sell order at your Target price. In Webull: go to your position → place a sell limit order at your target. Now your trade is fully managed: stop protects you, target locks in profit.

🎯 Pro Tip: OCO Orders

Webull supports OCO (One-Cancels-Other) orders. This lets you set both your Stop Loss AND Take Profit simultaneously. When one triggers, the other automatically cancels. Look for "OCO" in the order type dropdown — this is the most efficient way to manage your S.E.T.

Module 4: Position Sizing Practice STOCKS

  1. Pick any stock on your Webull watchlist
